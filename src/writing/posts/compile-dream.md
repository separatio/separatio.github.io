**COMPILE / DREAM** is a small book of ten poems about writing software in the age of AI. It reads as a terminal-green, page-turning flip-book: turn the leaves with the arrow keys, the page edges, or a swipe.

[→ Open the book](/writings/compile-dream.html)

## How it was made

This piece was made with Claude, end to end, as a multi-agent workflow using an **advisor pattern**:

- The premise was drawn from **current online discussion about AI and the craft of programming** — what people are actually arguing about, hoping for, and grieving as the tools change.
- **Claude Haiku 4.5** drafted each poem.
- **Claude Opus 4.8** acted as the advisor: it graded every draft hard and sent the weakest ones back for a revision written against its own notes.

It ran live as a 44-agent orchestration. Nine of the ten poems were revised once; the booklet's self-assessed average came out at 6.95 / 10. The full credits live on the book's final page (`// EOF`).
