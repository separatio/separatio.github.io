## Why I rebuilt this

Most developer portfolios read like a résumé printed in a browser. They list tools — React, TypeScript, Docker — as if the compiler cared what you know rather than what you ship. I wanted something different: a place that communicates how I think, not just what I've done.

The design is a [blueprint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blueprint) — deep navy, gold hairlines, monospace everywhere. Cyanotype meets engineering drawing. It felt right for the kind of work I do: precise, system-level, built to be reproduced.

## The "AI-first" part

When I say "AI-first builder," I don't mean "uses Copilot for autocomplete." I mean treating AI as the execution layer for an entire development cycle. My current stack uses [Flowy](https://github.com/sqaoss/flowy) to coordinate parallel workstreams — separate git worktrees, independent agents, each handling a bounded slice of the problem. This site itself was built that way: four parallel workstreams (design system, landing, navigation, writing) landing into a single branch without stepping on each other.

The pattern that's emerged from this: quality as defect prevention. When an agent makes a change, you verify it in a running browser before it's staged. When a feature is "done," you load it headlessly and capture console errors before claiming success. This isn't paranoia — it's just the discipline that makes AI-assisted work trustworthy.

```ts
// What "AI-first" actually means: verifiable, not just fast
const result = await preview('/writing')
assert(result.consoleErrors.length === 0)
```

## What "More than software" means

Software is the medium, not the goal. The projects I care about are in the intersection of AI infrastructure, family tooling, and things that actually change how someone's day goes. Multi-tenant SaaS, multi-agent orchestration systems, family AI tooling that earns trust over time rather than requesting it up front.

> High agency doesn't mean moving fast and breaking things. It means owning the full system — the spec, the build, the deploy, the ops — and being accountable to the outcome, not just the pull request.

I write here to think through what I'm building, how I'm building it, and the occasional lesson that didn't make it into a commit message.
